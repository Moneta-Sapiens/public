2. What is Coin Entropy?

Coin Entropy is a scientific measurement of the "amount of information" in each coin — similar to how scientists measure energy in physical systems — that correlates 80-95% with market prices without using any price data.

more...
Imagine if you could look at every wallet and bank account for a currency and see exactly how the money is spread out and where it moved. For example, the Bitcoin blockchain makes this information publically available. Coin Entropy measures these patterns using the same mathematical principles that physicists use to understand energy and information. For example, when money is more evenly distributed, the entropy is higher and the currency is more valuable and stable, and when it's concentrated in few hands, entropy is lower and the currency is more volatile and vulnerable. This gives us an objective, absolute, and universal way to determine what a currency should actually be worth, regardless of what people are willing to pay for it on any given day.

3. What problem does this solve?

Coin Entropy solves the fundamental problem that no one really knows what money is actually worth—we only know what people are willing to pay for it right now, which changes constantly due to emotions, manipulation, and market conditions.

Currently, coin prices fluctuate wildly based on news, emotions, rumors, and manipulation by large players. Investors and institutions have no reliable way to know if they're buying at a fair price or if a currency is truly undervalued or overvalued. This creates massive inefficiencies, bubbles, and crashes. Coin Entropy provides the missing piece: an objective, scientific baseline that shows what currencies should be worth based on their fundamental mathematical properties, allowing for truly informed investment decisions.
